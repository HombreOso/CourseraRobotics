Input csv:

"bodies_static_mass_properties.csv"
a description of the static mass properties of each of the 
N bodies: the (x,y) location of the center of mass and the total mass (e.g., in kg); 

and

"contacts_description.csv"
a description of the contacts. Each contact consists of a list of the two bodies involved 
in the contact (0 means stationary ground, 1 means body 1, 2 means body 2, etc.); the (x,y) 
location of the contact; the contact normal direction into the first body involved in the contact 
(this direction should be specified in degrees); and the friction coefficient 
μ at the contact. For example, in the image shown (C:\Users\admin\Documents\Robotics\Code\CourseraRobotics\2026-05-20_Example_image_assembly_statics.png), 
the single contact between body 1 and body 2 could 
be specified as (1, 2, 60, 60, 180, 0.5). This means the contact is between body 1 and 2, the 
contact location is at (60,60), the angle of the contact normal pointing into the first body in 
the list (body 1) is at 180 degrees, and the friction coefficient 
μ is 0.5. One of the contacts between body 0 (ground) and body 2 could be specified as 
(2, 0, 72, 0, 90, 0.25), indicating that the contact normal into body 2 is at 90 degrees 
and the friction coefficient is 0.25.
